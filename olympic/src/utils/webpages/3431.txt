German[edit]
  The inscription dem deutschen Volke ("to the German people") on the Reichstag.
Alternative forms[edit]
deudsch, deütsch, Deutsch, teutsch, teütsch, Teutsch (obsolete)
deutsh (rare, non-standard, obsolete)
Etymology[edit]


From Middle High German diutisch, diutsch, diutsc, tiutsch, tiusch, from Old High German diutisk, diutisc (“popular, vernacular”), from Proto-West Germanic *þiudisk, from Proto-Germanic *þiudiskaz (“of the people, popular”), an adjective from Proto-Germanic *þeudō (“people”) (compare Old English þeod), from Proto-Indo-European *tewtéh₂. Compare Dutch Duits, Low German düütsch, Icelandic þjóð. See also Dutch.
diutsc,

Adjective[edit]


deutsch (comparative deutscher, superlative am deutschesten)

German (of or about the German people)
Meine Mutter ist deutscher Herkunft, aber mein Vater ist Schwede.My mother is of German origin, but my father is a Swede.
German (of or pertaining to Germany)
der deutsche Außenminister — das deutsche Patentamtthe German foreign minister — the German patent office
German (of or pertaining to the German language)
einige deutsche Wörtera few German words
